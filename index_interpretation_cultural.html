Constructing Cultural Diversity Measures

The cultural diversity domains were informed by stakeholders meetings, workshops, available data sources, and existing literature on the capital. The cultural domains, religion and ancestry, are summarized using the following two measures.

1. Richness: The richness measure is the number of groups within the domain, in this case the number of religious groups or the number of different ancestries.

Interpreting Domain Measures

Choropleths display the value of the measures at the county level for the selected state. The boxplots below the choropleth display the county and city measures for each state. The filled circles over the box plot indicate the relative rurality of the county or city; the more saturated the shade of orange, the more rural, and the more saturated the shade of olive, the more urban a county or city is.

Index of Relative Rurality

We display variable boxplots by county relative rurality. We assign county rurality using Waldorf and Kim's 2010 Index of Relative Rurality (IRR). The IRR is based on population size, density, remoteness, and built-up area, and ranges from 0 (most urban) to 1 (most rural). Read more about the index here.
